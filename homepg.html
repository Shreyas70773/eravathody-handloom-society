Eravathody, a small village in Thiruvilwamala, Thrissur, carries a weaving legacy that dates back nearly 200 years. Brought here by the Raja of Kochi, Mudaliar weaving families from Tamil Nadu settled in the region to craft fine Kasavu garments for the royal household. Over generations, their skills evolved into a unique blend of Tamil and Kerala weaving traditions.

About the Cluster

Established in 1941, the Eravathody Handloom Weavers’ Co-operative Society now has 46 members and 19 looms, holding the Handloom Mark and known for its distinctive Kasavu borders and colour innovations. The society has earned district-level recognition, including honours for artisan Mr. Sundaram Mudali’s intricate Theyyam-inspired saree design.
