Est. 200 Years · Thrissur, Kerala

Woven through generations.

Eravathody, a small village in Thiruvilwamala, Thrissur, carries a weaving legacy that dates back nearly 200 years. Brought here by the Raja of Kochi, Mudaliar weaving families from Tamil Nadu settled in the region to craft fine Kasavu garments for the royal household.

The Living Craft · Kasavu Tradition

Threads of a royal heritage.

Each garment bears the signature gold zari border of Kasavu weaving — a tradition refined across generations. The loom is not merely a tool here; it is a living inheritance passed from father to son, mother to daughter, across two centuries of unbroken craft.

Tamil & Kerala · A Unique Fusion

Two traditions, one fabric.

Over generations, their skills evolved into a unique blend of Tamil and Kerala weaving traditions. This rare confluence gives Eravathody textiles their distinctive character — structurally precise yet flowing, rooted in heritage yet alive with innovation.
