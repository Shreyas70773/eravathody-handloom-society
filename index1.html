ABOUT THE CLUSTER

Established in 1941, the Eravathody Handloom Weavers' Co-operative Society now has 46 members and 19 looms, holding the Handloom Mark and known for its distinctive Kasavu borders and colour innovations. The society has earned district-level recognition, including honours for artisan Mr. Sundaram Mudali's intricate Theyyam-inspired saree design.

HISTORY

1941

The Eravathody Handloom Weavers' Co-operative Society is founded in the village of Eravathody, Thrissur district, Kerala.

1960S

The cooperative establishes its distinctive Kasavu border tradition, earning regional recognition for colour innovations.

1990S

Artisan Mr. Sundaram Muda develops the intricate Theyyam-inspired saree design, bringing national honours to the cluster.

Today

46 members. 19 looms. The Handloom Mark. A living archive of craft, labour, and cultural memory.
