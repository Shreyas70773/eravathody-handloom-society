HOW WE
GOT HERE

Nearly a century of weaving in one village — each decade a new chapter in the same unbroken story. Tap a year to read more.

1941
Founded +

The Eravathody Handloom Weavers' Co-operative Society is founded on 5th July in the village of Eravathody, Thrissur district. Mudaliar weaving families, brought by the Raja of Kochi nearly 200 years prior, form the cooperative's founding core.

1960s
The Kasavu Tradition +

The cooperative establishes its distinctive Kasavu border tradition, earning regional recognition for colour innovations. The golden zari border becomes synonymous with Eravathody cloth — a visual signature still carried today.

1980
New Premises +

The Society relocates to its current building at Kaniyarkode, Thiruvilwamala. The new premises house both pit looms and frame looms under one roof — a central weaving hall that the community continues to use today.

2002
Kerala State Honour +

Senior artisan Mr. Sundaram Mudali is honoured at the "Kalaparam Aaya Kaithari" event — a Kerala State Award — for his intricate Theyyam-inspired design woven into the mundani (pallu) of a saree. The honour brings national attention to the cluster.

2010
School Uniform Scheme +

Eravathody is included in Kerala Government's Handloom School Uniform Scheme, creating consistent large-scale demand. The Society weaves the highest number of uniforms in the district that year, stabilising income for its members.

2024
Kerala's No. 1 Handloom Society +

Recognised as Kerala's No. 1 Handloom Society by the Directorate of Handloom & Textiles and awarded ₹1,00,000. Today: 46 members. 19 looms. The Handloom Mark. A living archive of craft, labour, and cultural memory.
